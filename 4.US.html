Top 3 Highest Ranking Universities in the US


#1 M.I.T (Massachusetts Institute of Technology)

The Massachusetts Institute of Technology, abbreviated as M.I.T (Massachusetts Institute of Technology), is the most famous university for training technical experts. M.I.T is located in Cambridge, Massachusetts in the Northeastern United States. Founded in 1861 to meet the needs of the United States for industrialization, based on the model of an polytechnic institute and emphasizing laboratory teaching. M.I.T initially emphasized applied technology sectors at undergraduate and postgraduate level, and this helped to establish close cooperation with industrial companies.
M.I.T has been with and helping promote the digital age. In addition to the developments that paved the way for modern computing and computer network technology, MIT faculty, staff and students wrote some of the first interactive game programs such as Spacewar and created the great slang words and modern hacker culture. Many important computer-related organizations have been established here such as the GNU Project, Free Software Foundation. Unlike most universities in the world, at MIT, the number of graduate students is more than undergraduate students (accounting for about 60% of the total number of students). Many graduate programs are ranked among the top 10 programs in the United States.
Education program : Massachusetts Institute of Technology offers 46 major majors and 49 sub-majors, most notably Computer Science, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Engineering ...
M.I.T consists of 5 member schools: School of Architecture and Planning, School of Engineering, School of Humanities - Arts - Social Sciences, Sloan School of Management and School of Science.

#2 Princeton University


Founded in 1746 as a University of New Jersey, the Princeton University Institute ranked fourth in the four oldest universities in the US university system. Located in a small town in the state of New Jersey with not too big scale like many other prestigious universities, Princeton University has always been outstanding in academic circles and affirmed its "cathedral" position. Top excellent science in the world.

Princeton University offers a wide range of academic programs with more than 34 majors, of which highly regarded disciplines include Mathematics, Physics, Astronomy, Economics, History and Philosophy. In addition to bachelor training, Princeton University also focuses on graduate program training in many fields of Social Sciences, Natural Science and Humanities, especially Doctoral programs. Doctoral training programs often focus on developing personal talents and research capabilities of learners.

The faculty of more than 1,000 people including highly qualified management, training and scientific research and rich teaching experience have trained many famous Nobel Prize winners. Fields award-winning mathematicians and successful billionaires in many fields.


Famous figures who studied at Princeton University: former President Woodrow Wilson and James Mandison; Chairman of the US Federal Reserve FED Ben Bernanke is the former Dean of Economics at Princeton University; Genius physicist Albert Einstein also lived and worked during his final years at this prestigious school.

#3 Yale University



Founded in 1701, Yale University is located in New Heaven City, Connecticut, is one of the oldest universities in the United States, just behind Harvard University (1636) and William & Mary University (1693). Yale University is ranked 3rd in the top 100 top US universities according to the evaluation of US News & World Report. In recent years, Yale has maintained this position, standing behind or at the same level with Princeton University or Harvard University. With the current system, there are 12 affiliated schools specializing in teaching undergraduate and postgraduate programs and plans to establish two new schools and Yale University in the future with more than 1,100 teachers in charge of the work, teaching and counseling for more than 5,000 undergraduate students and 6,000 graduate students.

The school has many programs and research topics for students, especially famous in the Faculty of Arts and Sciences, Theology, Forestry and Environment, Law, Business Administration, Medicine ... According to statistics. Yale University is one of the top universities with the lowest admissions rate, at about 6.8% of the total. Most students who attend the school are those with outstanding talents and outstanding academic achievements. Celebrities who attended Yale University: American Presidents William Howard Taft, Gerald Ford, George H.W. Bush, Bill Clinton and George W. Bush; Supreme Court judges Sonia Sotomayor, Samuel Alito and Clarence Thomas; US Secretary of State Hillary Rodham Clinton, Cyrus Vance, and Dean Acheson; Democratic presidential candidate John Kerry ...
